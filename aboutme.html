Background:
I was born and raised in Elk Grove Village, Illinois, a Northwest Suburb of Chicago. During my childhood, I had two general activities that I loved. I loved sports, and I loved making movies.
I played (or tried) just about every sport there was. Hockey was my favorite and I played for 8 years until I stopped my freshman year of high school. From there I picked up cross country and track. I developed well over my four years and ended high school as a 5-time All-State athlete and a 2-time All-American. I committed to run at the Division 1 collegiate level at the University of Illinois, where I was also accepted to study computer science.
Oddly enough, making movies was how I came to love computer science. Every since I was really young, I would ask my parents about how movies were made. I used to think that they were shot in only one take, and I would always wonder how these modern marvels were pieced together. I would make terrible but imaginative short films with my brother when I was really young. When I reached junior high I became really interested and taught myself how to make and edit films by watching YouTube. From junior high to high school I became very good at using the tools of the adobe creative suite to create video effects and get the results I wanted.
